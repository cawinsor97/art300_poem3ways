THE PROMISE

by JANE HIRSHFIELD

the promise

Stay, I Said

to cut the flowers

They bowed

their heads lower.

Stay, I said to the spider

who fled.

Stay, leaf.

It reddened

embarrassed for me and

itself.

Stay, I said to my body.

It sat as a dog does,

obediant for a moment

soon starting to tremble.

Stay, to the earth

of riverine valley medows,

of fossiled escarpemnts

of limestone and sandstone

It looked back

with a changing

expression, in silence

Stay, I said to my loves.

Each answered,

Always.
